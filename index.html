About Me

I am an ambitious student with a passion for leveraging Artificial Intelligence to make a significant impact on the world. I strive to maintain a logical and analytical approach to decision-making, both in academic endeavors and in everyday life.

On the other hand, I am a caring person who wants to make the most of every situation. I am not hesitant to step up and take initiative, and I am always looking for ways to positively influence my life, and that of others. I enjoy using AI as a tool for innovation to enhance well-being and address social challenges. I am eager to learn more, so I can contribute more.

Currently, I am a student researcher at the Hasson Lab, Neuroscience Faculty at Princeton University, where I develop and apply computational methods at the intersection of AI and neuroscience. My work focuses on how language is processed and represented in the human brain using naturalistic electrocorticography (ECoG) data.
